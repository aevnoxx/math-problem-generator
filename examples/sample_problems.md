## Problem 2

**Type:** Derivative  
**Difficulty:** Medium  

Find the derivative of:

$$f(x) = - 3 x^{3} - 2 x^{2} - 2 x + \sin{\left(x \right)} - 1$$

### Solution

$$- 9 x^{2} - 4 x + \cos{\left(x \right)} - 2$$

### Steps

- Given function: f(x) = -3*x**3 - 2*x**2 - 2*x + sin(x) - 1
- Apply derivative rules:
- - Derivative of sin(x) is cos(x)
- Result: f'(x) = -9*x**2 - 4*x + cos(x) - 2
